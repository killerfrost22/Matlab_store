\section{Question 2}
\textbf{Assuming it is unique that we know that the least-squares solution is given by:$$
\hat{x}=\left(A^{T} A\right)^{-1} A^{T} b
$$What is mathematically wrong with the following attempt to simplify this? Specifically, which equals signs are not valid and why?$$\hat{x}=\left(A^{T} A\right)^{-1} A^{T} \bar{b}=A^{-1}\left(A^{T}\right)^{-1} A^{T} \bar{b}=A^{-1} \bar{b}
$$}

The least square solution is given by $\hat{x}=\left(A^{T} A\right)^{-1} A^{T} \bar{b}$
$$
\begin{aligned}
\hat{x} &=\left(A^{T} A\right)^{-1} A^{T} \bar{b} \\
&=\left[A^{-1}\left(A^{T}\right)^{-1}\right] A^{T} \bar{b} \text {, since }(A B)^{-1}=B^{-1} A^{-1} \\
&=A^{-1}\left[\left(A^{T}\right)^{-1} A^{T}\right] \bar{b}, \text { since matrix multiplication is associative } \\
&=A^{-1} I \bar{b} \\
&=A^{-1} \bar{b} \\
\hat{x} &=A^{-1} \bar{b}
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence there is no mathematical wrong in your attempt.
